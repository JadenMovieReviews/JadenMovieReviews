Fly Boys: 6/10

Flyboys, produced in 2006, is a World War I screenplay based on true events and people. The plot follows a group of American Soldiers who join the Laffayete Escadralle, becoming the first Amerian fighter pilots. It features some familiar names such as James Franco and Jean Reno, and is directed by Tony Bill.

Cons Pros
The movie can be inaccurate at times and only closely portrays the perspectives and narratives of the characters. The writers did a great job on transitioning between themes of romanticism and realism for the most part.
The CGI and greenscreen are done horrendously to the extent that it ruins the effect of immersion on the audience.
 The pacing is done very intentionally in a way that develops the characters and relationships.
The presentation of the personal narratives are so corny and cliche that they feel like filler scenes.
 Jean Reno, and Jennifer Decker pulled off great performances.
The characters were very two-dimensional, all given a very basic background story to forcefully inform the audience of.
 Ignoring the close-up shots, the fight scenes were incredible and less predictable than other action films.

Summary:
Tony Bill provides the a prime example of how to spend a $60 million budget and end up with one hour of great action scenes and one hour of cliches and forced exposition you wish you could skip. The actors' talents are wasted with these simple characters and a script full of simple yet somewhat effective corny fights and subplots. The character development and backstories are extremely basic, but are pulled off by the amazing cast. It's not the greatest war movie every to grace theaters, but it has its merits.
